Types Of Turtles / SRI LANKA

TYPES OF TURTLES/SRI LANKA

Green sea turtle

The green sea turtle is a large marine reptile found in tropical & subtropical seas around the world.They areknown for their green-colored fat and cartilage,hence the name"green sea turtles"They can grown up to 5 feet in length and weigh up to 700 pounds.Green sea turtles have a flattened streamlined shell that helps them swim easily inknown fore their distinctive green colored flesh and fat,which is due to their herbivorous diet of sea grass and algae.Unfortunately,green sea turtles are endangered due to habitat loss,pollution,hunting and the destruction of their nesting sites bu humans.

Loggerhead sea turtle

Logger sea turtles are one of the largest species of sea turtles and can weigh up to 350 pounds (159kg)and grow to be about3 feet (1m)in length.They have a distinctive large head with powerful jaws that are used to crush their prey,which mainly consists of carbs,mollusks,and other hard-shelled animals.Loggerhead can be found in the Atlantic ,Pacific and Indian Oceans,as well as the Mediterranean sea.They are known for their long migration,which can cover thousands of miles each year.

Leatherback sea turtle

Leatherback sea turtles (Dermochelys coriacea) are the larges of all sea turtles,reaching up to 2 meters (6.5feet) in length and weighing up to 700 kilograms (1500pounds).They are named after their unque leathery shell which lacks bony scutes and is covered in a layer of tough,rubbery skin.They feed mostly on jellyfish, but also eat octopus and tunicates."Life History" They mate in tropical coastal waters near nesting beaches, where females lay their eggs in the sand.


Olive ridley sea turtle

The Oliver Ridley is the smallest of the sea turtles species,reaching a maximum size ofabout 2feet in length and weighing up to 100 pounds.They are found in warm waters in the Pacific,indian,and atlantic Ocean.They are known for their symchronized mass-nesting events called arribadas,where hundreds of thousandsonly about 2 feet (60 cm)in length and a weight of 100 pounds (45cm).They are found in warm waters of the Atlantic,Indian and Pacific Ocean.

Hawksbill turtle

Hawksbill turtles are a critically endangered species of sea turtles that inhabit coral reefs in tropical and subtropical waters around the world.They are named fortheir distinctive hawk-like beak,which they use to feed on sponges and other invertebrates.Hawkbill turtles face many threats,including habitat loss,hunting for their meat and their beautiful shell,accidental capture in fishing gear,and pollution.In recent years,climate change has also become a major threat to their survival,as coral reefs are particulary vulnerable to the warming and acidification of the ocean.

Sri Lankan star turtle

Sri Lankan Star Tortoises is the same species (Geochelone elegans) as that found in India and Pakistan but has a specific geographic identity,They have been highly sought after in the illegal pet trade, especially in the South East Asian countries.The Sri Lankan tortoise is medium-sized, reaching up to 15 inches. The stark yellow lines that categorize the species increases their value. They live on average, 40-80 years in the wild, and eat a diet of mainly grasses: they have also been known to feed on insects and carrion.

Black pond turtle

The black pond turtle, also known as the spotted pond turtle or the Indian spotted turtle, is a species of freshwater turtle endemic to South Asia. It belongs to the monotypic genus Geoclemys.The spotted turtle is omnivorous, feeding on aquatic plants, small fish, snails, worms, slugs, spiders, tadpoles, and small crustaceans. Interestingly, this species will only feed under water. Spotted turtles emerge from hibernation in early spring, usually in March, and begin looking for mates.

Red eared slider turtles

The red eared slider gets its name from the small, red stripe around its ears, or where its ears would be, and from its ability to slide quickly off rocks and logs into the water. This species was previously known as Troost's turtle in honor of an American herpetologist Gerard Troost.Red-eared slider turtles may have been imported illegally and pose a exotic diseases and parasites risk to human and native wildlife health
